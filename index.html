What is Diabetes?

Diabetes is a chronic medical condition that occurs when the body is unable to effectively process food into energy. This is primarily due to issues with insulin, a hormone produced by the pancreas that regulates blood sugar levels. There are two main types of diabetes:

Type 1 Diabetes: This occurs when the immune system attacks the insulin-producing cells in the pancreas, leading to little or no insulin production. It often develops in childhood or adolescence.

Type 2 Diabetes: This is the most common form, occurring when the body becomes resistant to insulin or the pancreas fails to produce enough insulin. It is often associated with lifestyle factors and usually develops in adults, though it can also occur in children and teenagers.

How to Control Diabetes

  • Healthy Eating: Choose a balanced diet with fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and lean proteins. Avoid sugary and high-fat foods.
  • Regular Exercise: Engage in physical activities like walking or swimming to manage weight and improve insulin sensitivity.
  • Monitoring: Regularly check blood sugar levels to stay within the target range and adjust your diet or medication as needed.
  • Medication: Use insulin or oral medications as prescribed by your healthcare provider if needed.
  • Stress Management: Practice stress-reducing techniques such as mindfulness or yoga to support overall health.
